Seventh Grade History focuses on Global Revolutions and Power Struggles

⪼Georgetown Day School Seventh grade history tackles the theme of power by considering the ways in which institutionalized systems of power can be broken down or revamped. Global revolutions and power struggles are the focal point as we begin the year discussing early modern imperialism.Students then study the Haitian Revolution, the struggle to implement fair and permanent government thereafter, and the Haitian immigrant experience of the modern day. In the study of each region and major revolution, we contemplate the influences of geography and cultural exchanges, and attempt to identify challenges or obstacles to stabilizing post-revolutionary societies

⪼We will continue this practice of considering the historical colonization, revolution, and present day experience for several regions throughout the world, including Cuba, South Asia and the Middle East.We seek to stimulate curiosity and critical thinking in an active classroom. While exploring revolutions is inherently interesting, our focus centers on the process of careful historical study: thinking analytically by crafting questions and searching for evidence, and then corroborating that evidence with a number of sources.

⪼Class work includes small and large group discussions, special projects, simulations, debates, and research.
Our mission of global competence prompts us to attempt to investigate the world and make connections.Students recognize multiple perspectives and create a year-end performance-art piece; combining dance,drama,research, and video. production, in a year-end advocacy project. ⪼2015 Power Performance
